# Introduction

The vision of future 6G wireless communication is to provide fast, reliable, and seamless connectivity across the world. Traditional wireless networks mainly depend on ground-based cell towers, but they cannot always provide strong coverage in remote areas, oceans, mountains, disaster zones, or highly mobile environments. Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks, also known as SAGIN, aim to solve this problem by combining terrestrial base stations, UAVs, drones, high-altitude platforms, and Low Earth Orbit satellites into one connected network.

SAGIN is important because it can extend communication services beyond the limits of normal ground networks. By connecting space, air, and ground layers together, SAGIN can support applications such as smart transportation, emergency communication, Internet of Things services, remote sensing, autonomous systems, and future 6G mobile services. However, this type of network is much more complex than traditional wireless systems because satellites, UAVs, aerial platforms, and mobile users are constantly moving.

This dynamic environment creates many technical challenges. SAGIN must manage changing signal strength, limited spectrum, interference between different network layers, unstable links, traffic congestion, energy limits, and frequent handoffs between satellites, aerial platforms, and ground stations. It also needs to support large numbers of users and devices while maintaining quality of service, fairness, privacy, and security. These challenges make manual or fixed network management methods difficult to use in future 6G systems.

Artificial Intelligence can play an important role in solving these problems. AI techniques such as machine learning, deep reinforcement learning, multi-agent reinforcement learning, federated learning, and generative AI can help SAGIN make intelligent decisions in real time. These methods can support traffic offloading, task scheduling, UAV trajectory optimization, resource allocation, network slicing, load balancing, cooperative mobile access, and seamless handover. AI can also help satellites, UAVs, and ground networks coordinate more efficiently when network conditions change.

Security and physical-layer optimization are also important parts of AI-empowered SAGIN. Since space, air, and ground networks share wireless resources, they may face problems such as jamming, spoofing, eavesdropping, authentication risks, and cross-tier interference. AI-based methods can support physical layer security, continuous authentication, beamforming, interference suppression, and spectrum sharing. New technologies such as reconfigurable intelligent surfaces and edge AI can further improve communication reliability and reduce delays in non-terrestrial network environments.

Recent research also explores advanced ideas such as cybertwin-enabled SAGIN, generative semantic communication, agentic AI, and quantum multi-agent reinforcement learning. These technologies show that future SAGIN systems may not only connect users, but also intelligently manage communication, computing, sensing, security, and service quality together. For example, cybertwins can create virtual models of the network for better monitoring, while semantic communication can reduce bandwidth usage by focusing on the meaning and quality of transmitted information.

This project focuses on AI-Empowered Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks for future 6G wireless communication. The goal is to study how AI can improve spectrum sharing, resource management, trajectory optimization, beamforming, security, federated learning, task offloading, and seamless handoffs across space, air, and ground network layers. Through this survey, we aim to understand the current research progress, identify open challenges, and discuss future directions for building reliable, adaptive, secure, and intelligent SAGIN systems.

# Introduction

Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks, also known as SAGIN, are becoming an important part of future 6G wireless communication systems. SAGIN combines different communication layers, including satellites in space, airborne platforms, unmanned aerial vehicles, drones, and ground-based cellular networks. The main purpose of SAGIN is to provide seamless, reliable, and global connectivity, especially in areas where traditional terrestrial networks are limited or unavailable.

Traditional wireless networks mainly depend on ground-based infrastructure such as cell towers and base stations. However, these systems may not provide strong coverage in remote areas, oceans, mountains, disaster zones, or highly mobile environments. SAGIN helps solve this problem by connecting space, air, and ground communication systems into one integrated network. This makes it possible to improve coverage, reduce communication gaps, and support advanced applications such as smart cities, emergency response, autonomous transportation, military communication, and Internet of Things services.

Although SAGIN offers many benefits, it also introduces several technical challenges. These networks are highly dynamic because satellites, UAVs, drones, and users are constantly moving. This movement can affect signal strength, network stability, and connection quality. Other challenges include spectrum management, signal interference, beamforming, network security, and seamless handoff between different network layers. Managing these challenges manually is difficult because SAGIN systems are large, complex, and constantly changing.

Artificial Intelligence can play an important role in solving these problems. AI techniques can help SAGIN systems make smart decisions in real time. For example, AI can be used to optimize spectrum sharing, predict network traffic, improve beamforming, detect security threats, and support smooth handoff between satellites, airborne platforms, and ground networks. By using AI, SAGIN can become more adaptive, efficient, and secure.

This project focuses on AI-empowered SAGIN for future 6G wireless networks. The goal is to study the architecture of SAGIN, understand how AI can improve communication performance, and explore important areas such as spectrum optimization, beamforming, physical layer security, and seamless signal handoff. Through this research, we aim to understand how AI and SAGIN together can support the development of intelligent and reliable next-generation wireless communication systems.

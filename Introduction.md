# Introduction

Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks, also known as SAGIN, are an important part of future 6G wireless communication systems. The main idea of SAGIN is to connect terrestrial base stations, UAVs, drones, high-altitude platforms, and Low Earth Orbit satellites into one integrated network. This type of network can provide wider coverage and better connectivity in places where traditional ground networks are limited, such as remote areas, oceans, disaster zones, mountains, and highly mobile environments.

Although SAGIN can improve global communication, it also creates many technical challenges. Unlike traditional networks, SAGIN includes different network layers that move and change constantly. Satellites have fast orbital movement, UAVs have limited energy, and ground users may move between different coverage areas. Because of this, the network must handle problems such as changing signal quality, limited spectrum, interference, unstable links, traffic congestion, and frequent handoffs between space, air, and ground layers.

Artificial Intelligence can help manage these challenges by allowing the network to make smarter and faster decisions. AI techniques such as machine learning, deep reinforcement learning, multi-agent reinforcement learning, federated learning, and generative AI can be used for traffic offloading, task scheduling, UAV trajectory planning, resource allocation, network slicing, load balancing, and cooperative access management. These methods are useful because SAGIN systems need to adjust quickly when network conditions, user demand, or node positions change.

Another important part of AI-empowered SAGIN is reliable and secure communication. Since satellites, UAVs, and terrestrial networks may share wireless resources, interference and security risks become major concerns. AI can support spectrum sharing, beamforming, interference suppression, physical layer security, authentication, and threat detection. New technologies such as edge AI and reconfigurable intelligent surfaces can also help improve communication quality, reduce delay, and protect non-terrestrial network links.

Recent research also explores advanced 6G ideas such as cybertwin-enabled networks, semantic communication, agentic AI, and quantum multi-agent reinforcement learning. These approaches show that future SAGIN systems may not only provide connectivity, but also intelligently manage communication, computing, sensing, security, and service quality together. For example, cybertwins can help monitor and model the network, semantic communication can reduce unnecessary data transmission, and multi-agent learning can support coordination between satellites, UAVs, and ground stations.

This project focuses on AI-Empowered Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks for future 6G systems. The goal is to study how AI can improve dynamic spectrum sharing, trajectory optimization, resource management, beamforming, security, federated learning, task offloading, and seamless signal handoffs across heterogeneous network tiers. Through this survey, we aim to understand current research progress, identify open challenges, and discuss future directions for building more reliable, adaptive, secure, and intelligent SAGIN systems.

# Paper Summary

## Full Citation

**Full Citation:** Y. Xu and Q. Yu. "Deep Reinforcement Learning Based Computation Offloading and Resource Allocation Strategy for Maritime Internet of Things." *Computer Networks*, vol. 264, 2025. 
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.comnet.2025.111221

## Summary

This paper proposes a deep reinforcement learning approach for computation offloading and resource allocation in Space-Air-Ground-Sea Integrated Networks (SAGSIN) to support the Maritime Internet of Things (M-IoT). The authors combine mobile edge computing (MEC) with a software-defined networking (SDN) architecture to improve communication and computing efficiency in maritime environments. They introduce a Dueling Double Deep Q-Network (D3QN) algorithm that intelligently decides where computation tasks should be processed while efficiently allocating communication and computing resources. The simulation results show that the proposed method achieves lower task delay, reduced energy consumption, and better overall system performance than DQN, Double DQN, Dueling DQN, and Q-Learning.

## Relevance to Our Survey

This paper is highly relevant to our survey because it demonstrates how artificial intelligence can improve resource management in Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks. The proposed D3QN algorithm uses AI to make intelligent computation offloading and resource allocation decisions, helping reduce latency, lower energy consumption, and improve the overall efficiency of the network. These techniques support the development of reliable and intelligent 6G SAGIN systems.

## Critical Comment

The proposed framework shows strong performance in simulation and demonstrates the benefits of using deep reinforcement learning for maritime networks. However, the evaluation is limited to simulated environments and does not consider practical challenges such as changing weather conditions, satellite and UAV mobility, communication failures, or cybersecurity threats. Future work should validate the approach in real-world deployments and investigate how the model performs under dynamic network conditions.

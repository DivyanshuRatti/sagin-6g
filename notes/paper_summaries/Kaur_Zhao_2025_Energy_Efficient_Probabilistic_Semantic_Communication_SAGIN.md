# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:** Z. Zhao, Z. Yang, M. Chen, C. Zhu, W. Xu, Z. Zhang, and K. Huang, “Energy-Efficient Probabilistic Semantic Communication Over Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks,” IEEE Transactions on Wireless Communications, vol. 24, no. 10, pp. 8814–8829, 2025. DOI: 10.1109/TWC.2025.3569102.

## Summary

This paper investigates how probabilistic semantic communication can reduce energy consumption in a Space-Air-Ground Integrated Network. The proposed system uses a UAV to relay information from a satellite to multiple ground terminals while allowing the satellite and UAV to compress less important parts of the transmitted data. The ground terminals recover missing information using shared probability graphs, but this process requires additional computing resources. The authors develop an iterative optimization method that balances communication energy, computation energy, bandwidth, power, semantic compression, and UAV positioning.

## Relevance to Our Survey

This paper is relevant to our survey because it demonstrates how intelligent semantic processing can improve the energy efficiency of future 6G SAGIN systems. It also shows that communication and computing resources must be managed together rather than optimized separately. This supports our discussion of AI-assisted resource allocation, UAV relaying, and efficient information transmission.

## Critical Comment

One limitation is that the results are mainly based on numerical simulations and assume that all communicating devices share accurate probability graphs. Maintaining the same knowledge base across satellites, UAVs, and ground terminals may be difficult when network conditions and information change rapidly. Future research should test the method using real mobility patterns, imperfect knowledge bases, and practical UAV battery restrictions.

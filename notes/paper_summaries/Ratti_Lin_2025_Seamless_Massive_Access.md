# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:** Z. Lin, Z. Feng, K. Guo, A. Nauman, D. Niyato, and J. Wang, "AI-Driven Seamless and Massive Access in Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks," *IEEE Wireless Communications*, vol. 32, no. 3, pp. 72–79, 2025. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1109/MWC.001.2400371

## Summary

This paper studies how AI can help very large numbers of users access a SAGIN without causing poor service quality. In these networks, users may move between terrestrial, aerial, and satellite coverage, while the network topology also changes. The paper discusses AI-based methods for access control, resource allocation, mobility support, and network management. Its main goal is to make connections more seamless and scalable for future 6G services.

## Relevance to Our Survey

This paper is closely related to our objective of investigating seamless signal handoffs. It explains why AI is needed when many users, satellites, UAVs, and base stations have to work together. It can support our literature review and discussion sections on mobility, massive access, and future 6G connectivity.

## Critical Comment

The paper presents a broad AI-driven direction, but large-scale real deployments are still limited. Future work should test how well these methods handle fairness, privacy, security, and network congestion when many users request service at once.

# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:** F. Tang, C. Wen, X. Chen, and N. Kato, "Federated Learning for Intelligent Transmission With Space-Air-Ground Integrated Network Toward 6G," *IEEE Network*, vol. 37, no. 2, pp. 198–204, 2023. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1109/MNET.104.2100615

## Summary

This paper explains how federated learning can support intelligent transmission in SAGIN. Instead of collecting all data in one place, satellites, aerial platforms, and ground devices can train models locally and only exchange model updates. The paper discusses how this can reduce privacy concerns and lower the amount of raw data sent through the network. It also explains the challenges caused by different device capabilities, limited communication time, and highly dynamic links.

## Relevance to Our Survey

This paper is useful because federated learning is already part of our project's background and literature review. It gives a clear example of why decentralized learning is valuable in a network that includes satellites, drones, and ground devices. It can help us explain the privacy and communication advantages of federated learning.

## Critical Comment

The paper presents an important direction, but federated learning is not automatically efficient. Model updates can be large, devices may have unequal data, and satellite links may not always be available. Future work should compare the real communication cost of federated learning with centralized learning in practical SAGIN deployments.

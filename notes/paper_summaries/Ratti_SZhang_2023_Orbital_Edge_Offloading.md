# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:** S. Zhang, A. Liu, C. Han, X. Liang, X. Xu, and G. Wang, "Multiagent Reinforcement Learning-Based Orbital Edge Offloading in SAGIN Supporting Internet of Remote Things," *IEEE Internet of Things Journal*, vol. 10, no. 23, pp. 20472–20483, 2023. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1109/JIOT.2023.3287737

## Summary

This paper focuses on the Internet of Remote Things, such as sensors placed in remote areas where normal cellular coverage is weak or unavailable. The authors study how tasks can be offloaded to orbital edge resources through a SAGIN. They use multi-agent reinforcement learning, meaning that several AI agents learn and cooperate when making offloading decisions. The aim is to reduce delay and use network resources more efficiently.

## Relevance to Our Survey

This paper is relevant because one of SAGIN's main benefits is service in remote areas. It shows how AI can help satellites and other nodes support IoT devices that cannot rely only on ground infrastructure. It also gives us an example of decentralized AI, which connects to our discussion of multi-agent and autonomous network management.

## Critical Comment

Multi-agent reinforcement learning can become difficult to manage when there are many satellites, UAVs, users, and tasks. The paper mainly uses simulations, so more work is needed to show that the approach can scale and remain stable under real satellite movement and changing communication links.

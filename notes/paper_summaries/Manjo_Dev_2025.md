# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:** K. Dev, S. A. Khowaja, K. Singh, E. Zeydan, and M. Debbah, "Advanced Architectures Integrated with Agentic AI for Next-Generation Wireless Networks," arXiv preprint arXiv:2502.01089, 2025. https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.01089

## Summary (3–5 sentences in your own words)

From reading the paper, I understood that the authors are looking at different ways to improve future 6G wireless networks by using Agentic AI and new network architectures. Their goal is to make networks easier to manage, reduce operating costs, and support new services as wireless technology continues to grow. They discuss ideas such as AI agents that can learn and make decisions in real time, serverless computing for managing backend services, and optical networking to provide faster communication with lower delay. Overall, the paper focuses on making future wireless networks more efficient, flexible, and capable of handling increasing network demands.

## Relevance to Our Survey

I think this paper connects well with our survey topic because future Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks (SAGIN) will need smarter ways to manage communication between satellites, drones, aircraft, and ground stations. Since these networks are constantly changing, using AI to monitor the network and make decisions automatically could improve performance and reduce the amount of manual management. Some of the proposed architectures, such as AI agents and low-latency networking, could also help support the fast communication required in future 6G environments.

## Critical Comment

One thing I noticed is that the paper mainly introduces new architectural ideas instead of showing extensive real-world testing. The concepts are interesting, but it is difficult to know how well they would perform in a large and complex SAGIN environment based only on the information presented. I think future work should include more practical testing with satellites, drones, and ground networks to demonstrate that these proposed architectures can work effectively under realistic conditions.
